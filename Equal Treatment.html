Gender Inequality

Welcome. This is a documentary diving into gender inequality's root causes. Continue reading to see and learn our brainstorming techniques - mind maps, five whys, fishbone diagrams, and starbursting - to generate solutions.

Gender inequality is when people are treated differently based on their gender, usually favoring one over the other. It’s like saying boys or girls should do certain things just because of their gender, instead of letting everyone choose what they like and are good at. This isn’t fair, and everyone should have equal opportunities, no mater if they’re a boy or a girl. Gender inequality happens in various ways, like unequal pay or thinking that certain jobs are only for one gender. It’s important to treat everyone with respect and give everyone a chance to shine.

The Five Whys is a way to dig into the root causes of a problem. We started with questions and then asked why five times to get the root cause.

  1. What does gender inequality happen? Cultural expectations shape traditional roles.
  2. Why do cultural expectations shape gender roles? Societal norms perpetuate stereotypes about the role and capabilities of men and women.
  3. Why do societal norms perpetuate stereotypes? Historical power imbalances led to the establishment of patriarchy, resulting in gender inequality and women’s oppression.
  4. Why do these power imbalances establish Patriarchal Norms? Patriarchal Norms? Power imbalances in patriarchy lead to gender inequality and women’s oppression.
  5. Why do these power imbalances contribute to gender inequality? Patriarchal power fuels gender inequality, favoring men and limiting women’s opportunities.

The Fishbone, also known as Ishikawa or Cause-and-Effect diagram, helps explore the causes behind a specific issue. Each spine can be explored further to uncover specific causes within those categories. The Fishbone Diagram helps visualize and understand the connected factors contributing to gender inequality.

Starbusting is a way thoroughly explore a topic by asking Who, Why, How, and When questions.

Gender inequality is a issue that is deeply rooted in historical power imbalances, cultural expectation and discriminatory. It affects both men and women but disproportionately impacts women. While no single entity is solely responsible, societal norms and individual actions play crucial roles. The normalization of gender inequality underscores the need for continuous efforts to challenge stereotypes, promote inclusivity, and advocate for policy changes. Addressing this issue requires a multifaceted approach, including education, open discussions, and active efforts to create equal opportunities. As we strive to eliminate gender inequality, fostering awareness and understanding of its historical context and perpetuating factors is essential for building a more equitable and fair society.
